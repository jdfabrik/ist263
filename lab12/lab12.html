Mercury

Mercury is the smallest and innermost planet in the Solar System. Its orbit around the Sun takes 87.97 Earth days, the shortest of all the planets in the Solar System. It is named after the Greek god Hermes, translated into Latin as Mercurius, god of commerce and messenger of the gods.

Venus

Venus is the second planet from the Sun. It is named after the Roman goddess of love and beauty. As the second-brightest natural object in the night sky after the Moon, Venus can cast shadows and is occasionally visible in broad daylight.

Earth

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and the only astronomical object known to harbor life. About 29% of Earth's surface is land, and the remaining 71% is covered with water, primarily oceans.

Mars

Mars is a terrestrial planet with a thin atmosphere. Its surface features include impact craters, valleys, deserts, and polar ice caps, similar to Earth's.

Jupiter

Jupiter is the fifth planet from the Sun and the largest in the Solar System. It is a gas giant, two-and-a-half times the mass of all other planets combined, and is one of the brightest objects visible to the naked eye.

Saturn

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and the second-largest in the Solar System. Known for its extensive ring system, it is a gas giant with a mass 95 times that of Earth.

Uranus

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun. Its name references the Greek god of the sky, Uranus, the grandfather of Zeus (Jupiter) and father of Cronus (Saturn).

Neptune

Neptune is the eighth and farthest-known Solar planet from the Sun. It is the densest giant planet and has a mass 17 times that of Earth, slightly more massive than Uranus.
